The Story

A split season, swept clean.

Scuderia Practice 🍤 was formed when the 2024 family split brought Matthew Riley across from Practice 🍤 to lead a new entry into the 3GTUK Championship. Riley arrived with a Drivers' Championship already to his name and a reputation for converting strategic patience into results — exactly the foundation the new team was built around.

Alongside him sat Suhas Umashankar, whose hunger for the result matched Riley's composure stride for stride. The pairing took the wheel of a Ferrari 296 GT3, and the partnership clicked immediately: when the 2024 3GTUK season was run as two halves rather than one, Scuderia Practice 🍤 won both of them — two Constructors' Championships from a single season, and a second Drivers' title for Riley in the process.

Two halves, two Constructors' titles, one driver collecting his second crown — Scuderia Practice 🍤's campaign was as complete as a season gets.

Behind the two drivers, the team's engineers and strategists made sure the small margins were never left to chance — every gear calibrated, every strategy call backed by preparation. It's the same setup-first philosophy the whole Prawn GP family was built on, just with a Ferrari badge on the nose this time.

With the split-season campaign complete, Scuderia Practice 🍤's silverware now sits in the wider family's history books, while the team's lead role in carrying the 🍤 forward passes to 🍤 GP.
